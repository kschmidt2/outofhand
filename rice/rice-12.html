Sports Illustrated began its coverage with a short, straight-forward post on the suspension. Chris Burke followed up with a column blasting the NFL for the suspension, saying the league views smoking marijuana or taking Adderall as more serious crimes than allegedly knkcking your fiancee out cold. Burke pointed out that commissioner Goodell had a chance to put his foot down, but instead did the opposite. He calls on Goodell to step away from black-and-white league rules and "approach his job with some semblance of a human element." The next day, SI's Monday Morning Quarterback blog posted a column in which they said Rice "caught a major break ... except in the court of public opinion." This story also compared the suspension to the league's drug suspensions before discussing what could be expected of Rice in the upcoming football season.

ESPN's reporting on the suspension was more straight-forward. The first story was a straight news piece featuring quotes from both Rice and Goodell regarding the suspension. The article concluded with a summary of the monetary implications for Rice as well as the implications for the Ravens' offense. A few days later, they reported on the NFL's senior vice president for labor policy saying he didn't think the suspension was too light. Some of ESPN's on-air talent, such as Michelle Beadle and Keith Olbermann, criticized the NFL, but ESPN's online reporting did not offer commentary.
