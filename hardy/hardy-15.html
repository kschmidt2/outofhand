OCTOBER 25, 2015

Deadspin covered the fights with Bryant and Bisaccia in separate posts and highlighted Jones’ comments after the game: “He’s, of course, one of the real leaders on this team and he earns it and he earns it with respect from all of his teammates and that’s the kind of thing that inspires a football team.” The following day, Deadspin published a story titled “The Cowboys Are Just Going To Keep Covering For Greg Hardy.”

Sports Illustrated and ESPN covered the altercations in a similar fashion, with ESPN including more quotes from coaches and players dismissing, or even encouraging, the behavior. The following day, Sports Illustrated’s MMQB posted a column arguing the Cowboys’ Hardy experiment had gone awry.

The day after the game, ESPN’s Skip Bayless defended Hardy, saying “I don’t have a major problem with what Greg Hardy did, I only have a major problem with when he chose to do it.” Deadspin published a post in response asking, “why would anyone defend Greg Hardy?” The post’s author, Greg Howard, pointed out there’s no scenario where it’s okay to fight your coworkers.
